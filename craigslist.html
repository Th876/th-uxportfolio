Craigslist, A Smarter Sign-In

Project Overview

Craigslist has long been a go-to for classifieds, but its outdated sign-in flow leaves many users frustrated, leading to drop-offs and missed transactions.

Context

3-person UX team,
Arizona State University

My Role

UX Designer: User persona, user flow,
low- & mid-fidelity wireframes

Timeline

1-week sprint | May 2024

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Canva,
Adobe Creative Suites

Why This Redesign Matters

  • The Challenge: Confusing login flow, limited guidance and support, and lack of personalization.
  • Design Objective: Design a simplified, user-friendly sign-in flow that reduces friction, provides guidance, and personalizes the onboarding experience.

Figure 1. Before-and-After Sign-In Flow: Shows the shift from Craigslist’s outdated, poorly aligned form to a modern user-friendly design
with improved usability.

Exploration & Discovery

To understand the pain points in Craigslist’s sign-in flow, my teammates analyzed competitors like OfferUp, Facebook Marketplace, and Poshmark to identify intuitive onboarding patterns such as social login and guided prompts.

Figure 2. Competitor Analysis: Identifies onboarding strategies that inspired simplified steps and personalized prompts.

We also conducted remote interviews with three Craigslist users, uncovering common frustrations: confusing steps, limited guidance, and a lack of immediate support. From this, I created a representative user persona to guide the redesign, focusing on reducing friction and boosting confidence for first-time users.

Figure 3. User Persona: Showcases design decisions in real user behavior.

UX Design Process

User Flow

The frustrations uncovered in our user research directly shaped my contributions to the team. Using these insights, I created a user flow that simplifies each step and adds meaningful personalization, guiding users smoothly from login to task completion.

Figure 4. User Flow: Reduces friction and improves clarity.

Wireframes

Low-Fidelity

I created the sign-in steps to test early ideas for how users might move through the flow.

Figure 5. Lo-Fidelity Wireframes: Iterative exploration.

Mid-Fidelity

I polished the layouts by adding copy with personalized prompts and refining icons to transform the rough wireframes into a design that felt much closer to the final experience.

Figure 6. Mid-Fidelity Wireframes: Refinement to improve usability.

Style Guide

With the core research and wireframes complete, the team advanced my concepts into the visual and interactive redesign by defining colors, typography, and components.

Figure 7. Style Guide: Defines a cohesive visual identity that kept Craigslist familiar while supporting clarity and trust.

Final Design

With wireframes finalized, we translated the concepts into high-fidelity screens and an interactive prototype, with clear steps, and helpful prompts.

Figure 8. High-Fidelity Wireframes: Translates the designs into polished screens that made the flow easier to follow and reduced user errors.

Usability Testing

Testing confirmed that users experienced less confusion and completed sign-in faster, though some still needed quicker access to help.

Design Iterations

In response to this finding, I implemented an AI chatbot for instant guidance, personalized greetings, and a streamlined login, to deliver a clearer, faster, and more engaging sign-in experience.

Figure 9. Interactive Prototype: Simulates the complete personalized sign-in experience.

Key Learnings

  • Designing for legacy systems is different from designing from scratch: Modernizing Craigslist taught me how to balance innovation with familiarity so users don’t feel alienated.
  • Research isn’t just about pain points, it’s about opportunities: Interviews revealed hidden moments where personalization could build trust.
  • Collaboration shapes better outcomes: My individual contributions (persona, flow, wireframes) grew stronger when iterated with my teammates’ visual refinements.
