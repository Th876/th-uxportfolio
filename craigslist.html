Craigslist, A Smarter Sign-In

Project Overview

Craigslist has long been a go-to for classifieds, but its outdated sign-in flow leaves many users frustrated, leading to drop-offs and missed transactions.

Context

3-person UX team, Arizona State University

My Role

UX Designer: User persona, user flow,
low- & mid-fidelity wireframes

Timeline

1-week sprint | May 2024

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Canva,
Adobe Creative Suites

Why This Redesign Matters

The Challenge

Confusing login flow, limited guidance and support, and lack of personalization.

Design Objective

Design a simplified, user-friendly sign-in flow that reduces friction, provides guidance, and personalizes the onboarding experience.

Figure 1. Before-and-After Sign-In Flow: Shows the shift from Craigslist’s outdated, poorly aligned form to a modern user-friendly design
with improved usability.

Exploration & Discovery

Competitive Analysis

My teammates analyzed OfferUp, Facebook Marketplace, and Poshmark to identify effective onboarding patterns. We discovered that social login and guided onboarding were common, intuitive approaches.

From these observations, we realized the need for a simplified step structure and contextual prompts in Craigslist’s redesign.

Figure 2. Competitor Analysis: Identifies onboarding strategies that inspired simplified steps and personalized prompts.

User Interviews

To ground our redesign in real user needs, we conducted remote interviews with three Craigslist users. We described the sign-in as:

  • Confusing and redundant
  • Offering little guidance for first-time users
  • Lacking immediate support when stuck

UX Design Process

The frustrations uncovered in our user research directly shaped my contributions to the team.

User Persona

I created a persona representing a typical Craigslist user struggling with a time consuming sign-in process, and unclear guidance. This helped me focus our redesign on reducing friction and building confidence for first-time users.

Figure 3. User Persona: Showcases design decisions in real user behavior.

User Flow

From the persona, I created a sign-in flow that simplifies each step and adds meaningful personalization to guide users smoothly from login to task completion.

Figure 4. User Flow: Reduces friction and improves clarity.

Wireframes

Low-Fidelity

I sketched out the sign-in steps and where guidance should appear, testing early ideas for how users might move through the flow.

Figure 5. Lo-Fidelity Wireframes: Iterative exploration.

Mid-Fidelity

I polished the layouts by adding copy with personalized prompts and refining icons to transform the rough sketches into a design that felt much closer to the final experience

Figure 6. Mid-Fidelity Wireframes: Refinement to improve usability.

Bringing the Design to Life

With the core research and wireframes complete, the team advanced my concepts into the visual and interactive redesign.

Style Guide

To make the design feel cohesive, my teammates defined colors, typography, and components, keeping everything familiar to Craigslist users while supporting the clarity my UX decisions aimed for.

Figure 7. Style Guide: Defined a cohesive visual identity that kept Craigslist familiar while supporting clarity and trust.

High-Fidelity Mockup

We translated the wireframes into polished screens, emphasizing simplicity, clear steps, and helpful prompts. My early UX work guided the layout and flow, ensuring the interface was both usable and confident.

Figure 8. High-Fidelity Wireframes: Translated the designs into polished screens that made the flow easier to follow and reduced user errors.

Interactive Prototype

Next, the team brought the redesigned sign-in flow to life by building a fully interactive prototype.

Usability Testing & Insights

Then, we conducted a usability test which showed that users experienced less confusion and completed sign-in faster. However, testing also revealed a gap: users needed quicker help when stuck.

Final Design

Using these insights, I implemented an AI chatbot to provide users with instant assistance instead of burying help in an FAQ.

Our final design delivered clarity, speed, and personalization through the following implementations:

  • Simplified sign-in: reduced cognitive load and increased completion
  • AI chatbot: offered instant help for users when stuck
  • Personalized greetings: made users feel recognized and increased engagement

Figure 9. Interactive Prototype: Simulates the complete personalized sign-in experience.

Key Learnings

  • Small changes, big impact: Even small tweaks to the onboarding flow made a noticeable difference in reducing user frustration.
  • Personalization builds connection: Adding tailored prompts helped users feel more engaged and confident.
  • Rapid testing uncovers surprises: User feedback highlighted needs we hadn’t anticipated, like the demand for instant AI support.
  • Design is iterative: The flow isn’t final; it can continue to improve based on real user behavior.
