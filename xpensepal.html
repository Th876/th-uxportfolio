XpensePal, Simplifying Personal Finance
for Millennials & Gen Z

Project Overview

Managing expenses can be tough, especially for Millennials (Gen Y) (born between 1981 and 1996) and Gen Z (born between 1997 and 2012).
XpensePal is a mobile app I designed to simplify personal finance, helping users manage daily budgets, track long-term goals, and stay on top of their financial health.

Context

Solo Project

My Role

UX/UI Design

Timeline

2 weeks | August 2024

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Canva, Adobe Creative Suite,
Google Forms and Sheets

Why This Project Matters

  • The Challenge: Millennials and Gen Z struggle with complicated apps to manage budgets and long-term goals effectively.
  • Design Objective: Create an intuitive, supportive, and engaging personal finance mobile app.

Figure 1. XpensePal Screens: Showcases the splash, onboarding, dashboard, and budget screens.

Discovery & Research

Secondary Research Key Findings

  • Knowledge Gaps: Low financial literacy suggests XpensePal should embed interactive guidance.
  • Savings & Emergencies: Many users can’t handle financial shocks, highlighting the need for proactive savings features.
  • Day-to-Day Struggles: Debt and irregular income demand simplification and automation in daily budgeting.

Final Design

I translated mid-fidelity wireframes into high-fidelity mockups, and created a fully interactive prototype to simulate key tasks, including setting savings goals, updating budgets, and tracking debt.

Figure 12. High-Fidelity Screens: Showcases key screens like dashboard, budgeting, and savings.

Usability Testing

I then conducted usability tests with 5 participants to observe task completion, identify pain points, and gather actionable feedback for design improvements.

Design Iterations

Based on these insights, I implemented the following in the final design:

  • Rewards System: Allows users to share progress with friends/family, increasing accountability and engagement.
  • Share Milestones: for first-time users
  • Positive Alerts: Provides encouraging messages to reinforce good habits and reduce anxiety around finances.

Figure 13. Interactive Prototype: Demonstrates updated flows and milestone features.

Impact & Key Learnings

  • Guidance matters: Interactive tutorials with step-by-step support drove usability and boosted users’ confidence as 4/5 participants completed tasks independently.
  • User motivation is key: Milestone rewards and sharing features increased engagement; celebrating small wins reinforces habit formation.
  • Accessibility counts: WCAG-compliant colors, readable fonts, and adjustable settings improved usability, proving early accessibility prevents later redesigns.
